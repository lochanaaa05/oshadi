Emergency...

Emergency Unit is a medical treatment facility that specialises in the acute care of patients who arrive without prior appointment, either by their own means or by ambulance. In such circumstances the department must be prepared to provide initial treatment for a broad spectrum of illnesses and injuries, some of which may be life-threatening and require immediate attention. If you happen to require the services of an emergency department then no doubt your first thought will be about the efficiency and professionalism of the unit-because in an emergency, every second counts and every decision has potentially life-changing consequences.

At Life Care we understand your concerns only too well. Our 24-hour Emergency Unit is equipped with the latest technology, and is manned by skilled staff and medical professionals, including surgeons and anaesthetists. The Emergency Unit is well equipped to handle any type of medical or surgical emergency, including the care of trauma patients. You may need to go to Emergency for these severe or urgent conditions:

  • heart attack or stroke
  • loss of consciousness
  • problems with breathing or bleeding
  • injuries from accidents, physical assaults or falls
  • allergic reactions
  • drug overdoses or poisoning
  • severe burns

Administration...

Administration in our hospital plays a crucial role in ensuring the smooth and efficient operation of the entire healthcare facility.

The administrative team is responsible for managing the day-to-day opearations, including overseeing staff, managing budgets, and ensuring compliance with healthcare regulations. They work closely with medical and non-medical staff to coordinate services, improve patient care, and implement policies that enhance our hospital's overall performance. Additionally, hospital administrators handle strategic planning, resource allocation, and public relations, ensuring that our hospital remains responsive to the needs of its patients and the community it serves. Their leadership is essential in maintaining a high standard of care and facilitating a positive environment for both staff and patients.

Intensive Care...

Intensive Care Unit represents the highest level of patient care and treatment designated for critically ill patients with potentially recoverable life-threatening conditions.

Intensive care units cater to patients with severe or life-threatening illnesses and injuries, which require constant care and close supervision from life-support equipment and medication in order to ensure normal bodily functions. They are staffed by highly trained physicians, nurses and respiratory therapists who specialize in caring for critically ill patients. The ICU is different from other hospital wards in that:

  1. there are fewer beds.
  2. each bed has lots of equipment needed to care for very sick patients.
  3. fewer visitors are allowed.
  4. there are fewer patients for each nurse to look after-often there is one nurse for every 1 or 2 patients.
